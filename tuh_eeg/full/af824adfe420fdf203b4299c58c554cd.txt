CLINICAL HISTORY:  56 year old right handed man with seizures since childhood who had a recent seizure.

MEDICATIONS:  Dilantin, Phenobarbital.

INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  Photic stimulation and hyperventilation were performed.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.

TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm that at best reaches 9 Hz with low amplitude of 20-40 mV which is reactive to eye opening.  There is slightly increased amount of frontal central beta.  Sleep is characterized by stage I. Activating procedures with photic stimulation and hyperventilation produce no abnormal discharges.

ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:   None.

SEIZURES:    None.

IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.

CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
