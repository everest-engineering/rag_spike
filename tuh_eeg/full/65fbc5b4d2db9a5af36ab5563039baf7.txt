CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 57-year-old woman with end-stage renal disease and diabetes brought to the hospital with recurrent seizures and markedly elevated serum glucose.  Seizures are characterized by facial twitching.  Diabetes, hypertension and hepatitis C.
MEDICATIONS:
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed at the bedside using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one channel of EKG.  The patient was awake and extubated.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, there is a 9 Hz alpha rhythm, but a small amount of background theta noted.  Occasional FIRDA was noted.  Vertex waves were observed in Stage I sleep.
Heart rate 100 BPM.
IMPRESSION:  Abnormal EEG due to:
Excess theta.
Occasional FIRDA.
CLINICAL COURSE:  The FIRDA and theta are nonspecific findings, but are hallmarks of a metabolic encephalopathy.  No epileptiform features were identified.
