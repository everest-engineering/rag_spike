CLINICAL HISTORY:  29 year old right handed female with a history of anxiety, depression and scoliosis presents with episodes of staring with memory loss.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with addition of anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrodes.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures with photic stimulation and hyperventilation were performed.
TECHNICAL  DIFFICULTIES:  Some intermittent temporalis muscle artifact.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that at best reaches 9 Hz and is reactive to eye opening.  There is a normal amount of frontocentral beta.  Patient is recorded during wakefulness, sleep stage I and stage II.  Activating procedures with photic stimulation and hyperventilation produced no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
