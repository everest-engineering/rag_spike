REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 63-year-old man with a history of right shoulder injury who presents status post  seizures and fall.  Went to Episcopal, was discharged on Percocet.  On the way home had another seizure, now with wide based gait and rigidity.
MEDICATIONS:  Lorazepam, Serax, Tylenol.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with the addition of anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures were not performed.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record reveals a posterior dominant rhythm that appears to reach 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  However, he has a low amplitude.  There is a diffuse amount of beta activity seen throughout the recording.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures were not performed.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and drowsy EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and drowsy EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.  Please note that the excessive beta may be due to medication affects.
