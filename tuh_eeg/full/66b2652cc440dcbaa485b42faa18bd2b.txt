CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 57-year-old male with 10-year history of intermittent confusional spells, syncopal spells, loss of context and postictal confusion.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in the lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one channel of EKG.  Hyperventilation and photic stimulation were completed.
Technical quality was acceptable.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, there is symmetric, reactive, well-formed alpha rhythm with a small amount of frontocentral beta. Sleep was characterized by vertex waves with POSTS noted in stage II sleep.  Hyperventilation did not significantly activate the record Photic stimulation elicited scant driving.
Heart rate:  72 bpm.
IMPRESSION:  Normal EEG in wakefulness and sleep.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No epileptiform features were identified. If a diagnosis of epilepsy is consideration, additional EEG monitoring was suggested and was initiated.
