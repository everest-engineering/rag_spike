INTRODUCTION:
An EEG was performed using the 10/20 electrode placement system with the electrodes for the EKG and anterior temporal region. Wakefulness and sleep was recorded. Photic stimulation was recorded.
MEDICATIONS:
Metformin, Keppra, insulin, digoxin, and hydralazine.
TECHNICAL PROBLEMS:
None.
HISTORY:
87 year old man with HTN, DM, CHF, CAD and prostate cancer with episodes of loss of consciousness.
DESCRIPTION OF RECORD:
The record opened to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm at 9 to 10Hz, with an amplitude of 30 to 60 microvolts, which was reactive to eye opening. There was a moderate amount of frontal central beta waves. Sleep characterized by stage I and II. Photic stimulation revealed no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:
None.
SEIZURES:
None.
IMPRESSION:
Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:
No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen. Please note that a normal EEG does not exclude the diagnosis of epilepsy.
