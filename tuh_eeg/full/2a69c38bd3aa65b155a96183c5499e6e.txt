
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.

CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 27-year-old woman with episodes of seizures and anxiety who gets a headache, has lightheadedness and then passes out.  Sometimes she trembles and afterwards is confused.  Per mom, she was told she had seizures as anywhere and mom has also a history of seizures.

MEDICATIONS:  Tegretol.

INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and stage 1 sleep.   Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.

TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and stage 1 sleep.  However, stage 1 sleep is limited.  Activated procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation produced no abnormal discharges.

ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.

HEART RATE:  66.

IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.



CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
