CLINICAL HISTORY:  33 year old right handed female with history of syncopal episodes for 1 year.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Syncope.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system, additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures, including photic stimulation and hyperventilation, were performed.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm of a 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is a normal amount of frontal central beta.  The patient achieves stage I and stage II sleep.  Activating procedures produce no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  Note:  A normal EEG does not exclude the diagnosis of epilepsy.
