CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 31-year-old male with possible seizure and cocaine overdose.
MEDICATIONS:  Propofol drip, no others reported.
INTRODUCTION:  A challenging 20-minute digital video bedside EEG was performed using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel of EKG. The patient was agitated and moving about.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The EEG demonstrates a continuous pattern with diffuse beta.  Prominent 11 to 12 hertz activity is noted in the frontal regions.  Eye movements and other body movements were noted.
Sleep was not achieved.
Heart rate 96 bpm.
IMPRESSION:  This EEG is remarkable for,
Replacement of normal background with excessive beta.
Prominent frontal 11-12 hertz activity.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  There were no epileptiform features.  The pattern described above may reflect a pharmacologic pattern.
