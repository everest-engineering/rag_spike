CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is a 41-year-old woman with schizophrenia, migraine headaches, anxiety, and possible acute stroke with unilateral weakness and facial droop.  Recently diagnosed with epilepsy.
MEDICATIONS:  Percocet, Keppra, others.
INTRODUCTION:  Routine 20-minute digital video EEG was performed in the lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel EKG.  Photic stimulation was performed.  Hyperventilation was deferred.  Impedances were acceptable with the exception of T1-T2 electrodes.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, the overall background is well organized with a low voltage, 10 Hz alpha rhythm.  Drowsiness was characterized by rhythmic central theta.  Vertex waves, K complexes, spindles, and POSTs were noted in stage 2 sleep.
The patient in stage 2 sleep during photic stimulation, which did not otherwise significantly activate the record.
Heart rate 78 BPM.
IMPRESSION:  Normal EEG in wakefulness and sleep.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No focal or epileptiform features were identified.  If additional information about the patient's epilepsy is needed, a repeat, more prolonged EEG is advised.
