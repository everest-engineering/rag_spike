INTRODUCTION:
EEG was performed using 10-20 electrode placement system with an EKG of anterior temporal electrodes, The patient was awake and also in stage 1 and II sleep during this EEG record. Activation procedure was done with photic stimulations.
TECHNICAL PROBLEMS:
None.
HISTORY:
This is a 59-year-old man that presented on November 7, 2011, with a history of questionable seizure.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:
The record showed a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm at 9 to 10Hz with an amplitude of 30 to 60 mV, which was symmetric and reactive to eye opening. There was a moderate amount of frontocentral beta. Sleep was characterized by stage I and II with normal pattern. Photic stimulation revealed no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:
None.
SEIZURES:
None.
IMPRESSION:
Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:
Normal EEG with no seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen. Please note that normal EEG does not exclude the diagnosis of epilepsy.
