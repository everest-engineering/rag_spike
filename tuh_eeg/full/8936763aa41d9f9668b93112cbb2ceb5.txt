CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is a 77-year-old man with a history of psychosis and dementia as well as stroke, COPD, pneumonia, hypertension, hyperlipidemia.
MEDICATIONS:  Amantadine, aspirin, donepezil, Keppra, Lexapro, metoprolol, risperidone, trazodone, thiamin, simvastatin.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using standard 10-20 electrode placement system of anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrode.  Patient was recorded while awake and drowsy.  Activating procedures included and photic stimulation.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  There is no clear posterior dominant rhythm that can be seen or frontocentral beta. Some state changes can be seen. Photic stimulation produces no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  General slow waves of 2-4 Hz, amplitude of 2-60 mV in limited wakefulness and throughout sleep.
IMPRESSION:  Abnormal EEG due to generalized slowing.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This EEG reveals evidence of diffuse cerebral dysfunction, which is nonspecific with regards to etiology.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were clearly seen.
