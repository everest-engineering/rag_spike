CLINICAL HISTORY: 39 year old right handed male, mentally challenged, presented with 4 episodes of loss of consciousness, with low potassium.
MEDICATIONS: Unknown.
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel of EKG. Hyperventilation and photic simulation were performed.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: In wakefulness there is a 9 Hz posterior alpha rhythm with frequent eye flutter. Drowsiness is characterized by background slowing and POSTS. Neither hyperventilation nor photic stimulation activate the record.
HR: 72 bpm
IMPRESSION: Normal EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: No epileptiform features were seen. A normal EEG does not exclude the diagnosis of epilepsy.
