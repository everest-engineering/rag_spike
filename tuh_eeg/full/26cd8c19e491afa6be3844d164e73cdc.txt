REASON FOR STUDY:  Episode of loss of consciousness.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 33-year-old man with 2 episodes of loss of consciousness and fall.  The patient was told that his eyes were open, but was not responding.  He had shaking hands and feet.  One episode no warning, second one felt dizzy for a little while.  He has a brother with seizures and a past medical history of achalasia.
MEDICATIONS:  Effexor, Lunesta, Nexium, Claritin.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep.
Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:  The record opens to a low voltage posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  The patient is recorded during wakefulness and sleep, stage 1.
Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation which produced no abnormal discharges.
Abnormal discharges:  None.
Heart rate:  90.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
