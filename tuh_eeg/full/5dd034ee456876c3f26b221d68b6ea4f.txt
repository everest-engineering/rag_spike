CLINICAL HISTORY:  27 year old right handed male who presented with a diagnosis of headache described as sharp and visual disturbances with short term memory loss.
MEDICATIONS:  Motrin.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Rule out seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel of EKG.  Hyperventilation and photic stimulation are performed.  Wakefulness and sleep were recorded.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTY:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm at 9-10 Hz and an amplitude of 20-60 microvolts which is reactive to eye opening. There is moderate amount of frontocentral beta activity. Sleep is characterized by stage I and II with K complex and sleep spindles. Photic stimulation reveals no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen. Please note that a normal EEG does not exclude a diagnosis of epilepsy.
