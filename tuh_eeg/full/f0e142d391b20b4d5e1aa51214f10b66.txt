REASON FOR STUDY:  Episodes of seizures.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 30-year-old man with history of seizures for 3 months, approximately once a month.  Described as single tingling, then right upper extremity numbness and stiffness with loss of consciousness and confusion afterwards.  Past medical history of dementia.
MEDICATIONS:  Tegretol and Seroquel.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  The patient is recorded during wakefulness, stage I and stage II sleep.  Please note, the patient easily falls asleep throughout the record.
Activating procedures produced no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
HEART RATE:  66.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and asleep electroencephalogram.
