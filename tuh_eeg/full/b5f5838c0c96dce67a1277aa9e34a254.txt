CLINICAL HISTORY:  18 year old right handed female with a chief complaint of numbness and pain in the lower extremity as well as pressure in the head.  She also had an episode of bilateral eyes deviated to the right more than left with increased blood pressure, and went to the ER.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Questionable seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with the addition of anterior temporal single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm which reaches 9-10 Hz and is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  The patient is recorded in a wakefulness and stage 1 sleep.  Activating procedures put out normal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:   No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
