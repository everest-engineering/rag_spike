CLINICAL HISTORY: 65 year old right handed man with a past medical history of
epilepsy and experiencing intermittent staring spells.
MEDICATIONS: Levetiracetam, ASA, Benazepril, Simvastatin
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed In lab using standard 10-20
system of electrode placement with 1 channel EKG. Photic stimulation was performed.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: The tracing begins in a well-organized, awake
patient that exhibited 11 Hz alpha. A good deal of beta activity was seen. The
amplitude was low. There was some theta activity seen in the record as well. During
sleep the patient exhibited some increased beta as well as in stage I sleep there were
vertex waves and K complexes seen. No sharps visualized. During photic stimulation
there was driving seen.
IMPRESSION: Normal EEG
CLINICAL CORRELATION: There was no epileptiform discharges seen during the
study.
