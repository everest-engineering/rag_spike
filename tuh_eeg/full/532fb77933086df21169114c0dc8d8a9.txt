REASON FOR STUDY:  Rule out seizure.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is a 21-year-old woman who 3 months ago, before her menstruation, had an episode of confusion with right sided weakness and tingling of the face that lasted 1 hour.  She has a history of lupus and hypothyroidism.
MEDICATIONS:  Hydroxychloroquine, thyroid.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep, stage 1 and stage 2.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation are performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  There is some intermittent T5, T3, and L1 electrode artifact.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  The patient is recorded mainly in sleep and she goes into stage 1 and stage 2 sleep.  There is minimal wakefulness.
Activating procedures produce no abnormal discharges.
Abnormal discharges:  None.
Heart rate:  102.
IMPRESSION:  Normal EEG mainly asleep.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This EEG mainly in sleep is normal.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
