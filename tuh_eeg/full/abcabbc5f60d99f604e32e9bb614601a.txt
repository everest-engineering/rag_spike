CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 38 -year-old woman with a 2 year history of spells characterized by right neck pain and left chest pain followed by shaking of the left arm.  The patient had multiple spells today all with the same prodrome.  Some of the spells in the emergency room were followed by unresponsiveness.
MEDICATIONS:  Keppra
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in the lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one channel of EKG.   The patient was extremely drowsy.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, there is a 10 Hz alpha rhythm with a small amount of low voltage frontocentral beta activity.  Hyperventilation did not activate the record.  Photic stimulation was performed by the patient was in stage 2 sleep with no arousal produced brief alerting and a driving response.
Heart rate:  60 BPM.
IMPRESSION:  This is a normal EEG in wakefulness through stage 2 sleep.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No epileptiform features were noted.  The generous beta described above may be due to the use of benzodiazepines in the emergency room.   Due to the patient's repetitive seizures with impairment of awareness, long term monitoring was initiated.
