REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
MEDICATIONS:  Keppra and Lamictal.
HISTORY:  A 43-year-old woman with a history of seizures since 2007.  The patient describes the feeling of the seizure as feeling that the left side of her head is strange, then losses consciousness, and then is not sure what happens.  After the seizure, she is confused, has bitten her tongue, and she is disoriented.  They told that she has a generalized tonic-clonic seizure.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  The patient is recorded during wakefulness and sleep, stage I.
Activating procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation produced no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
HEART RATE:  66.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and asleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
