CLINICAL HISTORY:  49 year old right handed man with episodes during sleep.  He feels sleep derived.  He has trouble sleeping. He cannot move, but can hear and see, his tongue feels heavy, lasting 5 to 10 minutes.  Occurs 2 to 3 times per week.  The patient reports events like this during childhood.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Sleep disturbance.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with addition of anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrodes.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm at 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  Activating procedures produced no abnormal discharges.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness and drowsiness.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and drowsy EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
