REASON FOR STUDY:  Episodes of blacking out.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 51-year-old woman who presents with a history of blacking out.  She describes this episode as beginning after a fall with injury to a head.  She describes the blacking out events as feeling dizzy, then losing consciousness and being back to baseline after 1 to 3 minutes.  They began in March 2012.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrodes.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 10 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures produced no abnormal discharges including hyperventilation and photic stimulation.  Please note photic stimulation produced no photic driving.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
HEART RATE:  66.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and drowsy electroencephalogram.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and drowsy EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
