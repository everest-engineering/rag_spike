REASON FOR STUDY:  Change in mental status.

CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is an 83-year-old man with chief complaint of change in mental status, found wandering in a park.  The patient was found to have a myocardial infarct.

MEDICATIONS:  Famotidine, aspirin, Norvasc, and others.

INTRODUCTION:  A repeat EEG was performed using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with an addition of EKG.  The activating procedures were not done since the study was done at the bedside.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  At maximal wakefulness, the alpha rhythm is 8 Hz.  The patient gets drowsy with a slow rolling eye movement.  In sleep, there are vertex waves and K complexes.

Heart rate is 60 bpm.

Abnormal Discharges:  None.

Seizures:  None.

IMPRESSION:  This is a mildly abnormal EEG due to mild slowing of alpha rhythm.

CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No epileptiform features were seen and this is mildly abnormal study because of slow alpha rhythm.
