CLINICAL HISTORY:  46 year old woman with past medical history of seizures, first ones in 1989 and none until 2000, also headaches and asthma.
MEDICATIONS:  Norvasc, Tegretol, Tramadol, Hydrochlorothiazide, Flexeril, Fioricet, Buspar, Ambien/zolpidem, Simvastatin.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrodes.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and stage 1 and stage 2 sleep.  Activating procedures were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that is seen during the limited wakefulness that reaches 9 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is an excessive amount beta seen diffusely throughout the recording.  Activating procedures produced no abnormal discharges.  The patient goes into stage 1 and stage 2 sleep.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
