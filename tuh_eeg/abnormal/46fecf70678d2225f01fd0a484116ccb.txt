CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is a 52-year-old man with episodes of loss of consciousness with bladder incontinence and left-sided weakness.
MEDICATIONS:  Protonix, Aspirin, Hydralazine, Heparin, Benadryl.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Episodes of loss of consciousness.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep stage 1 and with minimal stage 2.  Hyperventilation and photic stimulation were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm of 9-10 Hz with an amplitude of 20-60 microvolts which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal amount of frontocentral beta.  Activating procedures with photic stimulation and hyperventilation produced no abnormal discharges.  Sleep stage 1 and a very mild amount of stage 2 is seen.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
