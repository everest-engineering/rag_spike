
CLINICAL HISTORY: 30 year old right-handed woman week 26 pregnant with episodes of seizures for 2 years with loss of consciousness and confusion.

MEDICATIONS: Prenatal vitamins.

REASON FOR EEG: Seizures.

INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system of anterior temporal and EKG electrodes. The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep. Photic stimulation are performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES: None.

DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: The record opens to a well formed organized background with a posterior dominant rhythm of 9 to 10 Hz with an amplitude of 20 to 60 microvolts, which is reactive to eye opening. There is normal frontocentral beta. During the recording the patient goes into stage I and stage II sleep. Activity and procedures with photic stimulation poses no abnormal discharges.

SEIZURES: None.

IMPRESSION: Normal awake and asleep EEG.

CORRELATION: No seizure or epileptiform discharges were seen.
