REASON FOR STUDY:  Change in mental status.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 34-year-old woman with worsening speech difficulties, trembling in the upper extremities, vertigo, generalized weakness for 2 to 3 weeks with a past medical history of hypertension, hyperglycemia, gastric bypass surgery and spinal stenosis.
MEDICATIONS:  Depakote, tramadol, Neurontin, insulin, vitamins.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation were not performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is some frontocentral beta.  Activating procedures including hyperventilation produced no abnormal discharges.  Again, the patient is recorded in wakefulness and stage 1 sleep, although sleep is limited during the recording.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
HEART RATE:  78.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and drowsy EEG.   No seizures or epileptiform activity was seen.
