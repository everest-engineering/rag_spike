CLINICAL HISTORY: 38 year old right handed female with history of anxiety,
depression, headaches presents with intermittent left-sided numbness and weakness.
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20
electrode placement system with additional EKG and anterior temporal electrodes.
Wakefulness and stage I and stage II sleep were recorded. Activating procedures
including photic stimulation and hyperventilation were performed.
MEDICATIONS: Naproxen, Lovenox, Aspirin.
REASON FOR EEG: Rule out seizures.
DESCRIPTION OF RECORD: The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant
rhythm of 9-10 Hz with an amplitude of 20-60 microvolts, which is reactive to eye
opening. Normal frontocentral beta is seen. Sleep stage I and stage II are seen.
Activating procedures with photic stimulation and hyperventilation produced no
abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES: None.
SEIZURES: None.
IMPRESSION: Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
Please that a normal EEG does not exclude the diagnosis of epilepsy.
