REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 38-year-old woman with seizures for a year while asleep.  The last one was on August 5, 2013 described as twitching, tongue biting, and disoriented afterwards.
MEDICATIONS:  Amitriptyline and colestipol.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with an additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The records opens a posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness, but mainly in sleep.  Note:  The patient drops off to sleep very quickly and is awake for a limited amount of time.
Activating procedures, including photic stimulation and hyperventilation produce no abnormal discharges.
Abnormal discharges:  None.
Heart rate:  90.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.  Note:  The patient does fall asleep quickly and this may indicate sleep disorder or medication induced effect.
