CLINICAL HISTORY: 67 year old female presented with syncope and toe ulceration.
MEDICATIONS: Vancomycin.
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20
system of electrode placement with 1 channel of EKG. Photic simulation was
preformed.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: The patient is drowsy throughout much of the
record. There is an 8 Hz posterior dominant rhythm identified. Features of drowsiness
including increase in beta followed by vertexes, K complexes, and spindles. With
arousal, the patient does achieve an 8.5 Hz alpha rhythm. Photic stimulation elicits
scant driving.
HR: 104 bpm
IMPRESSION: EEG within normal limits.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: No focal nor epileptiform features were observed in the
record. Normal EEG does not exclude a diagnosis of epilepsy.
