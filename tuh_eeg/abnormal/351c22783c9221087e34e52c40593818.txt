CLINICAL HISTORY:  69 year old right handed woman with past medical history of diabetes who was found down in Ophthalmology clinic.  The patient was evaluated for possible transient ischemic attack.  No seizure-like activity was observed.

INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed at bedside using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel EKG.  This is an awake, sleep study.  Photic stimulation was performed, however, hyperventilation was not.

The patient falls asleep quickly and there were vertex waves POSTs and K complexes.  Photic stimulation did not activate the record.  There is minor asymmetry as she drifts to sleep.
HR:    68 bpm
IMPRESSION:  Normal EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  There were no epileptiform features identified.  A normal EEG does not exclude a diagnosis of epilepsy.  If seizures are an important consideration, a follow-up study is suggested.
