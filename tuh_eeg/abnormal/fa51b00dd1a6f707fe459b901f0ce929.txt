CLINICAL HISTORY:  27 year old left handed man with history of seizures since age 13, presents with seizures that fluctuate mostly with medications and stress described as a right leg twitch, most of the right side, then loss of consciousness for 10-15 minutes.
MEDICATIONS:  Dilantin.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Photic stimulation activating procedures and hyperventilation were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm of 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  Sleep is characterized by stage 1 and activated procedures including photic stimulation and hyperventilation produced no abnormal discharges.  However, a nice hyperventilation response is seen.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
