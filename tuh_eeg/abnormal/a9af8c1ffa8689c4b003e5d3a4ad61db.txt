REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 35-year-old woman with a history of new onset seizure activity in July of 2012, described as head shaking and generalized body shaking lasting 5 minutes with biting her tongue afterwards and being confused for about 30 minutes.  The patient had another event on 08/29/2012, she complained of a frontal headache.  She has a family history of seizures.
MEDICATIONS:  Simvastatin, clonazepam, topiramate, Neurontin, omeprazole, Percocet, Tramadol.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with the addition of anterior temporal single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakeful and sleep.  Activating procedures includes  hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a posterior dominant rhythm at 10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is an excessive amount of theta throughout the recording.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness, stage I and stage II sleep.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.  There was no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  A normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.  Please note that the excessive theta may be seen as part of medication effect usually due to benzodiazepines or barbiturate effect.
