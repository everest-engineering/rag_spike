CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 47-year-old woman with slurred speech, facial droop, and numbness, recent dysrhythmia and TIA.
MEDICATIONS:  Toradol, Ativan, Cardizem, others.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in the lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one channel of EKG.  Photic stimulation was completed.  The patient was complaining of neck spasm and there is some muscle artifact noted in the EEG.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, there is a 10 Hz alpha rhythm but alpha squeak was identified.  There is also some 16 Hz beta frequency activity noted in wakefulness.  The patient transitions in and out of stage II sleep with vertex wave spindles and POSTS.
Photic stimulation elicited a bilateral driving response.
Heart rate 50 bpm.
IMPRESSION:  Normal electroencephalogram in wakefulness through stage II sleep.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No focal or epileptiform features were identified.  The patient was uncomfortable but drifting in and out of sleep.  Clinical correlation regarding underlying sleep deficit and/or somatic discomfort is suggested.
