CLINICAL HISTORY:  81 year old left handed man with history of end stage renal disease, hypertension, CVA, and Parkinson's, presents with a witnessed syncopal episode on March 24, 2012 after hemodialysis.
MEDICATIONS:  Heparin, ASA, Lipitor, Levodopa, Nitroglycerin.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Syncope.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures was performed, specifically photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm of 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontal central beta.  Sleep is characterized by stage I and stage II.  Activating procedures, which is photic stimulation, produces no abnormal discharges.  There is some intermittent mild bilateral mid temporal delta.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
