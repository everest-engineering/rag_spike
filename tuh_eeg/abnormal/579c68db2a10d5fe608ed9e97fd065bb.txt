CLINICAL HISTORY:  30 year old right handed female with a history of epilepsy.  She has partial seizures that are secondary generalized described as pressure in her head that crunches.
MEDICATIONS:  Carbamazepine
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed for 20 minutes using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and stage I, stage II sleep.  Activating procedures including photic stimulation and hyperventilation were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well-defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal frontocentral beta.  The patient is recorded in stage I and II sleep.  Activating procedures produce no photic driving and hyperventilation produces a diffuse slowing response.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and asleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:   No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
