CLINICAL HISTORY: 58 year old right handed man with change in mental status and visual hallucinations. The patient is admitted and treated for DT's, past medical history of seizures, hypertension, substance abuse.
MEDICATIONS: Dilantin, Phenobarbital, Amitriptyline, Clonidine, Dilaudid, Cepacol, Lovenox, Morphine, Geodon, Benadryl, Compazine
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed at bedside using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel of EKG. The patient is not cooperative. In addition, patient has periods of agitation with waxing and waning mental status.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: The overall background is slow and disorganized, with a mix of delta and theta, with transition to more theta, especially when the patient is moving around. With agitation, the records have increased disorganization and increase in delta especially when patient is agitated, is compatible with delirium. Patient is not cooperative and has irregular heartbeat with some PVC's. There is a small amount of beta in the record. Heart rate of this patient is 100.
IMPRESSION: Abnormal EEG due to:
I. Excess theta.
2. Increase in theta frequency when patient is agitated compatible with delirium.
3. Diffuse disorganization of the record.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: The background is slow in disorganization in addition to the increased theta with patient's agitation may be consistent with alterations of delirium. There is no spike and wave activity and there are no triphasic waves observed in this record.
