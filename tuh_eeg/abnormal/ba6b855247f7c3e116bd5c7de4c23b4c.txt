REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 47-year-old woman presented with a history of seizures since age 12 with an aura of smell, followed by tonic clonic seizing and confusion.  The last one was a month ago.
MEDICATIONS:  Topamax.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode. The patient was recorded during wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF RECORD:  The record reveals a posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is an excessive amount of beta seen throughout the recording.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness and stage 1 sleep.
Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation produce no abnormal discharges.
Abnormal discharges:  None.
Seizures:  None.
Heart rate:  78.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.  Please note that the excessive beta may be due, at least in part to medication effect.
