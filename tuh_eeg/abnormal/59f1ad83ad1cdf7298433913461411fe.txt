CLINICAL HISTORY: A 55-year-old gentleman with a history of dementia, depression and stroke who has had episodes of loss of awareness lasting 1-2 minutes occurring every 6-8 weeks.
MEDICATIONS: Synthroid, Zoloft, others.
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG is performed in the lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one channel of EKG. Photic simulation is completed.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: In wakefulness, the background EEG is well organized with 10.3 Hertz alpha rhythm of 40 microvolts. Prominent eye movement artifact is observed.
Drowsiness is characterized by anterior spread of the alpha rhythm.
Photic stimulation elicits scant driving. Heart rate 60 bpm.
IMPRESSION: Normal EEG in wakefulness.
CLINICAL CORRELATION: No epileptiform features are identified. If epilepsy is an important consideration a repeat study either captured sleep or one of his specific events may be useful.
