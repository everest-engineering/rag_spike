REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizure.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 39-year-old man with history of hypertension who presents to us with a single seizure described as shaking and loss of consciousness, stating that he took about 4 Tylenol because he could not sleep and he was drinking alcohol.
MEDICATIONS:  Metoprolol and amlodipine.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a well defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is normal amount of frontal central beta.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness and drowsiness.
Activating procedures, including hyperventilation, revealed no abnormal discharges.
Abnormal discharges:  None.
Seizures:  None.
Heart rate:  72
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and drowsy EEG.
