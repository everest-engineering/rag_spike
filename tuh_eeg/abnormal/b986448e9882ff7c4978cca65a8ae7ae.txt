REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizure.
CLINICAL HISTORY:  A 43-year-old man with one-time seizure, felt lightheaded, fell to the floor, had confusion, generalized tonic-clonic, shaking and confusion afterwards, has a history of hyperthyroidism, maternal aunt with seizures.
MEDICATIONS:  Propranolol, propylthiouracil.
INTRODUCTION:  A routine EEG was performed using the standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single-lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness and sleep.  Activating procedures included hyperventilation and photic stimulation.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  There is an excessive amount of beta seen diffusely throughout the recording.  The patient is recorded in wakefulness, stage 1 and stage 2 sleep.  Activating procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation produced no abnormal discharges.
Heart rate 72.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  This is a normal awake and sleep EEG.  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.  Please note that the excessive beta may sometimes be seen due to the effect of benzodiazepines or barbiturates.
