CLINICAL HISTORY:  This is a 43-year-old man who is in prison complaining of left facial twitching for 7 months.  Also reports occipital headaches.
MEDICATIONS:  None.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed in the laboratory using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement and 1-channel EKG and the patient had left facial twitching during the recording.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  In wakefulness, there is bilaterally symmetric alpha of 10 Hz frequency which is reactive to eye opening.
Sleep was not achieved during the recording.
Photic stimulation did not elicit any abnormal discharges.
The facial twitching had no EEG correlate.  This is not a seizure.
Heart rate:  80.
IMPRESSION:  This is a normal awake EEG.  The facial twitching had no EEG correlate.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  Normal EEG does not rule out a diagnosis of epilepsy.  The facial twitching recorded during the EEG was not a seizure.
