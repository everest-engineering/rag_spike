CLINICAL HISTORY:  42 year old right handed woman with history of hypertension, bipolar disease, history of schizophrenia, presents with having left arm and leg pain and numbness.
MEDICATIONS:  Oxycodone, Tramadol, ASA
REASON FOR STUDY:  Left arm and leg numbness.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video EEG was performed using standard 10-20 electrode placement system with additional anterior temporal and single lead EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness, stage I and stage II sleep.  Activating procedures were performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: The record displays a well defined posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 9-10 Hz which is reactive to eye opening.  The patient is recorded mainly in sleep and wakefulness.  Note:  The patient easily goes into stage I and stage II sleep seconds after closing her eyes. Activating procedures including hyperventilation and photic stimulation produce no abnormal discharges.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.
SEIZURES:  None
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:  No seizures or epileptiform discharges were seen.
Note:  The patient does have a very short sleep latency and clinical correlation should be performed for a sleep disturbance.
