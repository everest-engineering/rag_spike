CLINICAL HISTORY:  58 year old left handed man with history of seizures as a child, presents with several months of frequent episodes of falls, left leg weakness, as well as tonic clonic seizures.  He has a past medical history of a pacemaker, bilateral hip and knee replacements.
MEDICATIONS:  Androgel, Polyethylene, Glyco, Omeprazole, Lovastatin, Lisinopril, Coreg, Fenofibrate, Tramadol, Furosemide, Veramyst.
REASON FOR STUDY:  Seizures.
INTRODUCTION:  Digital video routine EEG was performed using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with additional anterior temporal and single lead  EKG electrode.  The patient was recorded during wakefulness and drowsiness.  Activating procedures, including photic stimulation was performed.
TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES:  None.
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD:  The record opens to a posterior dominant rhythm that reaches 10 Hz, which is reactive to eye opening.  There is a normal amount of frontal central beta.  Activating procedures, which was photic stimulation, produced no  abnormal discharges.  The patient was recorded in wakefulness, drowsiness, and stage I sleep.  Stage I sleep was very short.
ABNORMAL DISCHARGES:  None.  At times there is some central sharply looking theta, however, it does not appear to be pathologic.
SEIZURES:  None.
IMPRESSION:  Normal awake and sleep EEG.
CLINICAL CORRELATION:   No seizures or epileptiform discharges were clearly seen.
